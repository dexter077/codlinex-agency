GEO / AEO: Why It Matters Now
The Complete Business Guide

AI engines now answer 40% of Google queries directly — and most businesses are completely invisible to them. Here's why that's happening, what GEO is, and what to do about it.

01 · From Search Engine to Answer Engine

In 2023, a profound shift occurred in how people consume digital information. ChatGPT reached 100 million users faster than any product in history — faster than Google's own 20-year trajectory. Perplexity, Google AI Overview, Claude Search, and Gemini followed shortly after.

What unites all these systems is one thing: users no longer search — they ask.

When someone types "What's the best accounting software for my small business?" into Google, they get 10 blue links. When they ask the same question on ChatGPT, they get a direct recommendation — citing only sources the AI engine trusts.

Google AI Overview is now active in 40% of all Google searches as of 2026. Users in these queries click through to external sites at significantly lower rates — meaning even a top-10 Google ranking may deliver far less traffic than it once did.

02 · Why Traditional SEO Is No Longer Enough

SEO was designed around Google's link-based ranking algorithm. Backlink counts, keyword density, and page load speed determine where a page ranks. These tactics built entire industries — and they still work for Google.

But AI engines don't consult Google rankings. When generating an answer, an AI system evaluates completely different signals:

  • Is this content citable? Structured, sourced, and unambiguous?
  • Is this brand verifiable? Schema, entity signals, About page present?
  • Is this site open to AI crawlers? robots.txt, llms.txt configured correctly?
  • Is the content current and trustworthy? Dates, authors, external references?

A site optimized for Google for years can be completely invisible to AI engines. These two systems speak different languages — and most businesses haven't learned the second one yet.

03 · How AI Engines Select Content

Large language model-based search engines run a source selection process when generating answers. This happens in three distinct stages:

1
Crawl
The AI bot attempts to access your site. If robots.txt blocks it — even accidentally — the site is excluded from consideration entirely. Most businesses don't know their AI crawler settings.
2
Parse
Schema.org structure, llms.txt, and content hierarchy (H1→H2→paragraph) allow the AI to understand and categorize your content. Without this, the AI sees raw text with no context.
3
Cite
Content that provides reliable, sourced, and clear answers is selected as a citation source. This is what GEO optimizes for — being the source AI points to, not just a site AI can access.

If a business fails the crawl stage, no amount of content quality will get it cited by AI engines. This is a more critical barrier than any traditional SEO issue — and it takes under an hour to fix.

05 · The 8 GEO Categories

A GEO score is measured from 0 to 100 across 8 technical and content categories. Each category evaluates specific requirements that AI engines check when deciding whether to cite a source:

🤖 ROBOTS.TXT 18 pts

Manages access for 27 AI bots. GPTBot, ClaudeBot, or PerplexityBot may be accidentally blocked.

📄 LLMS.TXT 18 pts

Introduces your site to AI engines. Like a resumé for your content — what it covers and which pages matter most.

🧩 SCHEMA JSON-LD 16 pts

Page type, organization, content structure. Machines can only truly understand content with Schema markup.

🏷️ META TAGS 14 pts

Title, description, Open Graph, canonical. AI engines extract page summaries from these fields first.

📝 CONTENT STRUCTURE 12 pts

H1→H2 hierarchy, word count, external links, numerical data. Citability comes directly from structure.

📡 SIGNALS 6 pts

Language attribute, RSS feed, freshness dates. AI engines prefer content with clear temporal signals.

🔭 AI DISCOVERY 6 pts

/.well-known/ai.txt, /ai/summary.json, /ai/faq.json — the emerging AI discovery layer standard.

🏢 BRAND ENTITY 10 pts

About page, contact info, sameAs links. Knowledge Graph verification and entity disambiguation.
